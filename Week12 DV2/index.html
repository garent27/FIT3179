World Map of Fastest Solves

This map provides a bird's-eye view of the fastest official 3x3 Rubik's Cube solve ever achieved in each country. The deeper the shade of green, the faster the national record time. Click on a country to dive deeper and see its top 10 champions in the chart on the right.

Top 10 of Selected Country

This chart ranks the best individual 3x3 solves in seconds. By default, it shows the top 10 fastest solvers in the world. When you select a country from the map, this list updates to reveal the top 10 record-holders for that specific nation, offering a focused look at its local talent.

Fastest Solve Progression

Follow the year-by-year improvement in 3x3 Rubik's Cube solve times for the world's speedcubing powerhouses. This visual shows not only the fierce competition between nations but also the overall advancement of the sport. The clear drop in times highlights how new techniques and dedicated practice have shattered previous records.

Competition Solves by Event

Explore the breakdown of competitive solves for each official WCA event. In this treemap, the size and color intensity of each block represent the total number of solves for that puzzle type. This provides a clear and immediate comparison, highlighting the overwhelming popularity of the standard 3x3x3 cube.

Competition Activity

This heatmap reveals the explosive growth of speedcubing competitions across continents since 2010. The deeper the shade of blue, the higher the number of events held in that region for a specific year. You can instantly see the sport's incredible expansion in Asia and North America and the powerful rebound in activity worldwide after 2021 .

Gender Participation

These charts track the year-over-year growth in male and female participation for the most popular puzzle events. Each stacked bar shows the total number of competitors, highlighting both the explosive overall growth of the sport and the consistent gender ratio within the community. As expected, the classic 3x3 Cube attracts the largest and most diverse field of competitors.

New Competitors Growth

This chart visualizes the annual influx of first-time competitors, revealing the geographic waves of the sport's popularity. The stacked areas show how different continents have contributed to the cubing community's growth over time. Notice that covid-19 caused a huge drop in new participants but went back to normal after 2022.
